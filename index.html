Work

My journey as an AI developer has been driven by one simple goal — building intelligent systems that genuinely help people in their daily work. Whether it’s a mechanic in a garage, a doctor in a clinic, or a student in a classroom, I’ve focused on creating AI that feels practical, reliable, and intuitive.

At Valere, I led the development of Mech AI — a smart assistant that helps automotive technicians troubleshoot and fix problems using natural language. I also worked on Athes AI, a voice-enabled AI coach designed to support athletes with personalized recommendations based on speech input and real-time context.

Earlier at Hiteshi Infotech, I explored the ethical use of computer vision with an AI-based productivity tool that respected user privacy while offering smart analytics. I also built a conversational medical assistant that could interpret images and guide diagnoses, along with an AI teaching tool for medical students that made learning more interactive and adaptive.

Every project taught me something new — about people, about problem-solving, and about pushing the boundaries of what AI can do. I’m always excited to turn complex tech into something simple, meaningful, and impactful.
